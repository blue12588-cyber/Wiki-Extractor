Concept: Deliberate practice — a learning approach where the learner sets a
specific narrow goal, attempts it, and receives immediate corrective feedback
before re-attempting. The improvement loop is small, fast, and explicit.

Background

The author observed that informal repetition without feedback rarely produces
durable improvement. Setting explicit narrow goals and pairing them with a
short feedback signal closes the loop.

"Practice without feedback is rehearsal of mediocrity, not improvement."

The quotation above frames the rest of the document. It is short on purpose
so that it survives copying into citation contexts unmodified.

Closing

Deliberate practice is not the same as repetition. The presence or absence of
a feedback signal distinguishes the two.
