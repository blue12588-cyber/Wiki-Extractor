## Concept

**Deliberate practice** — a learning approach where the learner sets a specific
narrow goal, attempts it, and receives immediate corrective feedback before
re-attempting.

The improvement loop is small, fast, and explicit. Without the feedback signal,
the same repetition does not accumulate skill.

## Why it matters

Informal repetition without feedback rarely produces durable improvement. The
distinction is mechanical, not aesthetic.

> Practice without feedback is rehearsal of mediocrity, not improvement.

The blockquote above is the canonical formulation used elsewhere in this
fixture set. It is short on purpose so that downstream consumers can quote it
directly.

## Closing

Deliberate practice is not the same as repetition. The presence or absence of
a feedback signal distinguishes the two.
